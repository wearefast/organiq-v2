# SaaS Industry Strategy Template

## Strategic Priorities (SaaS-Specific)

1. **Product-Led Content** — Create bottom-of-funnel content targeting keywords with high commercial/transactional intent (e.g., "[tool] alternatives", "[tool] vs [competitor]", "best [category] software")
2. **Integration & Use-Case Pages** — Build landing pages around integration partners and vertical use cases to capture long-tail commercial traffic
3. **Educational Content Moat** — Invest in TOFU how-to and educational content that establishes brand authority in the problem space
4. **Programmatic SEO Opportunities** — Identify template-based page patterns (feature pages, comparison pages, glossary terms) that scale with minimal incremental effort
5. **Free Tool / Calculator Strategy** — Build free tools that attract links and traffic while showcasing product value

## Content Mix (Recommended)

| Funnel Stage | Content Type | Share of Effort |
|---|---|---|
| TOFU | Blog posts, guides, glossary | 35% |
| MOFU | Comparison pages, use cases, case studies | 35% |
| BOFU | Product pages, integrations, pricing, demos | 20% |
| Retention | Help docs, changelog, academy | 10% |

## Keyword Prioritization (SaaS Signals)

- **High priority:** Keywords with `transactional` or `commercial` intent + CPC > $3 (indicates buying intent)
- **Quick wins:** Keywords positions 4-20 where existing product/feature pages can be optimized
- **Link magnets:** Informational keywords with high volume that attract backlinks (studies, benchmarks, templates)
- **Competitor intercept:** "[brand] alternative", "[brand] vs [our brand]", "[brand] review" patterns

## KPI Benchmarks (SaaS B2B)

| Metric | 90-Day Target | 6-Month Target |
|---|---|---|
| Organic sessions | +25% | +60% |
| Demo requests from organic | +15% | +40% |
| Keyword positions in top 10 | +30% | +80% |
| Domain Rating improvement | +3 DR | +8 DR |
| Backlinks acquired | +50 referring domains | +150 referring domains |

## Risk Factors

- SaaS keywords are highly competitive; expect 3-6 months for competitive terms
- Product changes can break existing content; maintain a content audit cadence
- Avoid thin feature pages — each must have 800+ words of unique value
